Before You Animate

This tool uses a motion synthesis model to make still images move. The same technology family powers deepfakes, so we ask every user to agree to ground rules first.

  • No real people without consent. Do not animate photos of classmates, teachers, public figures, or anyone who has not agreed.
  • No deception. Anything you create here is for learning how the technology works, not for passing off as real footage.
  • Label what you share. If you post a clip somewhere, say it is AI-generated.

Animals, drawings, statues, and your own face are all fair game. Make a squirrel talk.

How It Works and Why We Teach It
  • The model. LTX-Video, a latent diffusion transformer: it encodes your image, then denoises a whole block of video frames at once, guided by your text prompt.
  • Why students should build one. Synthetic media is not going away. Understanding the pipeline from the inside is the most reliable way to learn its tells: warped backgrounds, inconsistent lighting, texture shimmer.
  • Spotting fakes. Look at ears, teeth, and hair during fast motion. Keypoint models struggle with occlusion and fine texture, and that is exactly where artifacts hide.
  • Our ground rules. Consent required for real people, no deceptive use, and this guideline stays on every visit.
